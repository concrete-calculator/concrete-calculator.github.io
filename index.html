What is the difference between cement and concrete?

+

Cement and concrete are often confused, but they're different materials. Portland cement (a hydraulic binder made primarily from limestone) is actually just one ingredient. The mix also contains sand, aggregate, and water. When these components combine, a chemical reaction called hydration occurs, forming a solid material that hardens and strengthens over time.

What aggregate should be used for concrete?

+

Choosing the right aggregate is important for performance. Use different particle sizes—fine sand and gravel are recommended. The ratio of sand to remaining aggregate should be 1:3. Before mixing, it's worth sifting through a sieve to remove impurities like peat, wood, or glass.

How long does concrete take to dry?

+

Under most conditions, freshly placed concrete will dry and become solid enough to walk on after 24 hours. However, it needs 28 days to fully cure and attain maximum strength, durability, and wear resistance.

How to prevent cracks in concrete?

+

If you're pouring new concrete, contractors can take several measures to significantly reduce cracking: use a low water-cement ratio mix, place it on a well-compacted subgrade, install control joints where needed, and properly cure the material. Learn more about causes

Can you resurface concrete?

+

Yes, resurfacing is a good way to revive old, worn, or damaged concrete surfaces like driveways, patios, or pool decks. It involves applying a thin cement-based overlay on the existing slab to cover cracks, pits, or other imperfections. You can choose different finishes - from simple broom texture to decorative stamped patterns or staining. Properly done, resurfaced concrete can last as long as new pour but at much lower cost than full removal and replacement. Just ensure hiring an experienced concrete contractor for professional results.

Is this concrete calculator 100% error-free?

+

Our concrete calculator uses precise, industry-standard formulas, so results are theoretically accurate. However, in real projects there can be normal loss and shape deviations from ideal models. You should always purchase a little more material than you need—we recommend allowing extra for waste, typically 5–10%, when ordering.
